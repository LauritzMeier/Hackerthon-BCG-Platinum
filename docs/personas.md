# Persona Library

## Purpose

This document defines a broad starter portfolio of personas for the longevity MVP.

It is intentionally more diverse than a single target persona so we can reason about:

- nationality and local expectations
- age and life stage
- digital fluency
- price sensitivity and willingness to pay
- different health motivations

## Important Note

The dataset does not contain income or wealth. Affluence tiers below are service-design assumptions used to shape pricing, packaging, and communication. They must not be treated as factual patient attributes unless validated by real business inputs.

## Persona Design Principles

- Cover prevention and chronic-risk use cases
- Span younger adults through older adults
- Include both price-sensitive and premium-preference segments
- Reflect the actual country spread present in the data
- Keep the product patient-facing and action-oriented

## Cross-Persona Insights

- Trust and clarity matter across every segment.
- Time poverty is as important as clinical risk for engagement.
- Wealth changes packaging, service tier, and channel strategy more than it changes the core coaching need.
- Younger segments respond to momentum and habit framing.
- Older segments respond better to reassurance, readability, and clinician-adjacent trust.
- High-affluence segments expect personalization and transparency.
- Price-sensitive segments need modular offers and visible value.
