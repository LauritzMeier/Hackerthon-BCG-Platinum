# ADR-0003: Persona Portfolio Before Single Persona Selection

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-04-09

## Context

The challenge requires a clear patient persona, but selecting one too early creates a risk of designing a narrow product without understanding broader European variation.

The data spans multiple countries and a wide age range. The product also needs to account for different affordability expectations and digital fluency levels.

## Decision

Create a broader persona library first, then select one primary MVP persona from that set.

The portfolio must cover variation in:

- nationality
- age
- life stage
- affluence and price sensitivity
- digital fluency
- health motivation

## Consequences

- Persona selection becomes an explicit decision instead of an assumption.
- The team can choose a primary narrative without losing awareness of secondary segments.
- Product design is more likely to generalize across European markets later.

## Alternatives Considered

- define one persona immediately
- build around the median dataset patient
- treat all patients as one generic “prevention user”
